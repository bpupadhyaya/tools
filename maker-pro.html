Why a local AI wins

Maker Pro doesn't race cloud assistants on raw horsepower. It competes on four things a shared, metered cloud service structurally can't do.

🧬 It learns you

A cloud model is the same for everyone. Maker Pro periodically fine-tunes your local model on your corrections, taste, and accepted work — it literally gets better at building your tools every week.

🔍 Sees everything, shares nothing

Cloud tools only know what you upload. Maker Pro can index your documents, notes, data, and code right on the machine — and none of it ever leaves.

♾️ Never meters you

Local inference is free, so Maker Pro spends compute extravagantly — parallel drafts, model ensembles, verify-and-repair loops — luxuries no per-token service can afford to give you.

🔓 Yours for good

No rate limits, no model deprecations, no outages. Works on a plane, in a clinic, on an air-gapped box. Your tools keep running whether or not you keep paying.

Benchmarks your machine and tunes quantization, context length, and GPU offload per model. Your hardware, fully used.

Parallel local workers
